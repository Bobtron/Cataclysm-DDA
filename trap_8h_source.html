89 /**
90  * Some traps aren't actually traps in the usual sense of the word. We use traps to implement
91  * funnels (the main map keeps a list of traps and we iterate over that list during rain
92  * in order to fill the containers with water).
93  * Use @ref is_benign to check for that kind of "non-dangerous" traps. Traps that are not benign
94  * are considered dangerous.
95  *
96  * Some traps are always invisible. They are never revealed as such to the player.
97  * They can still be triggered. Use @ref is_always_invisible to check for that.
98  *
99  * Traps names can be empty. This usually applies to always invisible traps.
100  *
101  * Some traps are always revealed to the player (e.g. funnels). Other traps can be spotted when
102  * the player is close (also needs perception stat).
103  *
104  * Use @ref map::can_see_trap_at or @ref trap::can_see to check whether a creature knows about a
105  * given trap. Monsters / NPCs should base their behavior on that information and not on a simple
106  * check for any trap being there (e.g. don't use `map::tr_at(...).is_null()` - that would reveal
107  * the existence of the trap).
108  */
